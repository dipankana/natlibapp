The National Library of India is the county’s largest library and the library of public record. The library "operates under the national government’s Department of Culture and is designated to collect, disseminate, and preserve all printed material produced in India, and all foreign works published about the country where ‘every work about India…can be seen and read’" (Murray, 2009). The National Library is a result of the merging of the public library with the Imperial Library several government libraries. The National Library (1953), then the Imperial Library housed several foreign (British) and Indian titles and was open to the public. (more)

The National Library is the apex body of the library system of India. Serving as a public library, it is a permanent repository of all documents published in India.

The Library has a collection of rare documents.

The Library has elaborate arrangements for physical conservation of books for posterity.

The website contains bibliographic information of a portion of the Library's holding with limited fields for ready access by users.

Arrangements have been made for registered users to view digitized books.
